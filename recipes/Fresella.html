Frisella

Frisella description

Frisella (also called "frisa", "fresella" or "friseddhra" in Salento) is a sort of biscuit-shaped bread in the shape of a donut
or tarallo typical of Apulian, Campania, Calabrian and Lucanian cuisine. It is a gastronomic product with durum wheat (or barley)
flour cooked in the oven, cut in half horizontally and toasted again in the oven. It follows that it has a porous and a compact face.
It is important to distinguish between frisa and bread: frisa is in fact not bread, as it is cooked twice.

Ingredients

  • Frisella
  • Oil
  • Cut Tomato
  • Tuna if you like

Preparation

  1. immerse the frisella in the water for a few seconds
  2. Squeeze it to remove excess water
  3. Add cut tomato on it
  4. Add some oil and salt on it
  5. If you like add tuna
